## What the lease usually says

In the Porto Metropolitan Area, urban leases often include clauses on **works and alterations**. Fixed split with embedded piping and an outdoor unit almost always counts as a permanent change.

**Portable** AC with a window exhaust sits in between: not structural, but noisy or ugly if poorly fitted. The safe path:

1. Describe the solution to the landlord (unit type, kit photo).
2. Confirm **no exterior wall drilling**.
3. Keep approval by email or message.

**Landlord email template:**

> Dear landlord, I plan to install portable air conditioning with a removable window kit in the bedroom/living room, with no facade works or permanent frame change. Installation is done by a technician and can be removed when I leave. Please confirm you have no objection.

## Condominium in Porto: what usually causes trouble

- **Split outdoor unit** on a street-facing balcony — often banned in minutes.
- **Poorly sealed duct** with visible foam — neighbour complaints.
- **Condensate dripping** onto the pavement — legal and neighbour issue.

Curated install fixes the last two: discreet finish and internal or collector drainage.

## Mistakes that cost your deposit

- Drilling granite or concrete to “fix the kit better”.
- Leaving glue marks on the window when removing.
- Overloaded extension leads — fire risk and paint damage.
- Ignoring night noise — neighbours in narrow Porto blocks complain fast.
