|------|---------------------------|
| **Arrendamento urbano** | Urban housing lease — governs works and improvements. |
| **Benfeitoria** | Improvement to the property; fixed split usually counts as one. |
| **Kit de janela** | Removable set sealing the window gap without permanent works. |
| **Senhorio** | Landlord who approves (or blocks) changes to the flat. |
| **Administrador de condomínio** | Building manager — inform before install. |

## What the lease usually says

In the Porto Metropolitan Area, urban leases often include clauses on **works and alterations**. Fixed split with embedded piping and an outdoor unit almost always counts as a permanent change.

**Officially:** Portuguese law distinguishes types of improvements — fixed AC tends to be a material alteration.

**In practice:** many Porto landlords accept a **reversible** solution if the facade is untouched and frames are unmarked.

**Portable** AC with a window exhaust sits in between: not structural, but noisy or ugly if poorly fitted.

Compare with split in our [portable vs split guide](/en/guides/portable-vs-split-ac-porto/). For full context, see the [complete Porto guide](/en/guides/portable-ac-porto-2026/).

## Condominium in Porto: what usually causes trouble

- **Split outdoor unit** on a street-facing balcony — often banned in minutes.
- **Poorly sealed duct** with visible foam — neighbour complaints in narrow Baixa blocks.
- **Condensate dripping** onto the pavement — legal and neighbour issue.

**In practice:** curated install fixes the last two — discreet finish and internal or collector drainage.

**Gap:** many tenants only contact the landlord and skip the building administrator. If bylaws mention «visible equipment», inform both.

## Mistakes that cost your deposit

- Drilling granite or concrete to «fix the kit better».
- Leaving glue marks on the window when removing.
- Overloaded extension leads — fire risk and paint damage.
- Ignoring night noise — neighbours in narrow Porto blocks complain fast.
- Installing in July without telling the landlord — conflict when the power bill arrives.

## What the law says (practical summary)

**Officially:** Portuguese civil code and the NRAU govern tenant improvements. Works altering structure or facade need agreement.

**In practice:** courts value **reversible** solutions and prior notice. A dated email before install is your best evidence.

**Gap:** «landlord did not reply» is not approval — send a follow-up and wait 7–10 days before booking.
