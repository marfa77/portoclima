## 30-second summary

Choose **split** if you own, have condo approval and can wait weeks for works. Choose **curated portable** if you rent, live in an older Porto block or need comfort **this summer** without an assembly vote.

## Comparison table — Porto and AMP

| Criterion | Curated portable (Porto Clima) | Fixed split |
|-----------|--------------------------------|-------------|
| Predictable upfront | €899–1,199 turnkey | €800–1,500+ (works + unit) |
| Timeline | 1 day | 2–6 weeks |
| Facade / balcony | No outdoor unit | Outdoor unit — often regulated |
| Rental | Reversible | Permanent works |
| Indoor noise | Moderate (Soundless line) | Low |
| Long-term kWh | Higher if poorly sealed | Lower (inverter) |
| Porto humidity | Good in one room | Good whole home |

## When portable is the better Porto choice

- Lease **under 24 months**
- Condo with history of **blocking facade splits**
- **Granite windows** hard to pipe for works
- **1-bed** where only one room needs cooling
- Need install **before the next heatwave**

## When split pays off

- Owner with **works already planned**
- **Written** condominium approval
- Staying **5+ years**
- Cooling **several rooms** with multi-split
- Budget for **certified HVAC** and annual service

## Hidden cost of DIY portable

A €350–500 retail unit looks cheap — but without a fitted window plate and insulation, Porto installs lose efficiency and gain street noise. Turnkey covers what the box omits: **survey, right BTU, finish and test**.

## Hidden cost of split

- Delayed condominium vote
- Piping in stone walls — works surprises
- Install warranty separate from equipment warranty
- Hard to remove on exit — deposit or resale impact for tenants

## Quick decision

```
Renting + summer already hot? → Curated portable
Owner + assembly OK? → Split
Unsure on condo? → Portable first
40 m² attic? → 2×12k or consider split long term
```
