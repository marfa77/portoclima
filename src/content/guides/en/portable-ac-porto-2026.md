## Why Porto is different from Lisbon for cooling

Porto has many pre-Pombaline and post-war buildings with **thick walls, narrow windows and granite frames**. They store daytime heat and release it slowly at night. In July and August, 32–38 °C with **Atlantic humidity** makes the air feel heavier than the thermometer shows — common in Cedofeita, Bonfim, Campanhã and the Ribeira.

**Officially:** IPMA records several days above 35 °C per season in Greater Porto.

**In practice:** ground floors by the Douro and top floors without shade feel peaks earlier than the city-wide forecast. We measure window, exposure and humidity on survey — not just floor area.

You do not need facade works to stay comfortable. A **portable air conditioner** with a sealed window exhaust solves most 1- and 2-bed flats — provided the kit is well insulated and BTU matches real heat load.

## Rental and condominium rules

- **Landlord:** most contracts require written consent for permanent changes. Removable window kits are often accepted — always confirm. Details in our [rental guide](/en/guides/portable-ac-rented-apartment-porto/).
- **Condominium:** if the unit does not change the visible facade, many Porto administrators do not require a vote. External units on balconies almost always do.
- **Listed buildings / Ribeira UNESCO zone:** prefer reversible solutions; we do not drill stone or granite frames.

**Common gap:** tenants assume «portable = zero rules». Noise, dripping and visible kits can still trigger complaints without facade works.

## Humidity, drainage and granite windows

In Porto, condensate must go to an **internal tank or fixed drain hose** — not onto the pavement. Basements and ground floors near the Douro have higher ambient humidity; our survey measures **heat load** (afternoon sun, attic, poor airflow).

Sliding or sash granite windows need a **custom adapter plate**. That is where cheap installs fail — hot air leaks in and the unit overworks.
